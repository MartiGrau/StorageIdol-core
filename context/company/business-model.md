# Business Model

## Revenue streams

| Stream | Description |
|---|---|
| Setup fee | One-time per client deployment (configuration, integration, testing) |
| Monthly platform fee | Recurring per module per client |
| Per-lead pricing | Variable revenue tied to lead volume (Module 2) |
| Recovery commission | % of debt collected (Module 3, optional) |
| SLA / support retainer | Optional add-on for priority support and change requests |

## Positioning

StorageIdol is **not** SaaS. It is a **technology deployment business**. Each client gets a dedicated configuration, integrated to their CRM and branded to their voice. This justifies a higher price point than a generic SaaS tool and avoids competing on a feature-comparison matrix.

## Target client size

- **Sweet spot:** 50–500 units / 200–2000 leads per month / €50k–€500k debt portfolio
- **Floor:** Below this, the ROI is marginal and the setup cost is hard to justify
- **Ceiling:** Above this, enterprise procurement cycles slow sales; target mid-market

## Unit economics (indicative)

- Setup fee: €2,000–€5,000 per client
- Monthly recurring: €500–€2,000 per client depending on modules
- Target LTV: 24+ months per client
- Target payback on CAC: < 4 months

## Growth motion

1. Land with one module (lowest friction, fastest value demonstration)
2. Expand to additional modules at renewal or at 60-day check-in
3. Referrals within property management and financial servicer networks (tight industry, word travels fast)
