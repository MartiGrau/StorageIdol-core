# Market Problems

## Problems we solve

### 1. Lost leads due to slow or absent follow-up
Real estate companies receive inbound leads (forms, missed calls, WhatsApp) and respond hours or days later. By then the prospect has moved on. A qualified lead that is not contacted within 5 minutes has a 21× lower conversion rate.

### 2. Human agents cannot scale repetitive outreach
Calling a portfolio of 500 tenants to remind them about a payment due is expensive, inconsistent, and demoralizing for human agents. The same conversation repeated hundreds of times is exactly what AI does best.

### 3. Debt collection is manual and inefficient
Most property managers and financial firms still rely on manual calls and generic emails for debt recovery. There is no systematic staging (D+3, D+10, D+20, D+30), no tone escalation, and no integrated payment option — all of which directly reduce collection rates.

### 4. 24/7 customer service is cost-prohibitive with humans
Tenants and buyers ask questions outside business hours. Companies either miss these inquiries or pay for expensive night-shift coverage. An AI agent handles 90%+ of FAQs with no incremental cost per conversation.

### 5. Multilingual support is a bottleneck
In markets with diverse populations (Spain, LatAm), companies struggle to serve clients in their preferred language. ElevenLabs-powered voice agents switch languages natively with no additional headcount.

## Who has this problem

- Property management companies (>50 units under management)
- Real estate agencies with active lead pipelines
- Financial firms or servicers managing debt portfolios
- Mortgage brokers with long nurturing cycles

## Why now

- WhatsApp API is now production-ready and accessible to SMBs
- Voice AI (ElevenLabs) has crossed the uncanny valley — callers can no longer tell it is a bot
- LangGraph enables reliable, stateful multi-agent workflows that were impossible 18 months ago
- Regulatory pressure on debt collection is increasing, making systematic, logged outreach safer than ad-hoc calls
